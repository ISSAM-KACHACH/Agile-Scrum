Core Concepts & Fundamental Values

Agile is a philosophy and framework for project management that focuses on flexibility, continuous collaboration, and iterative delivery. It breaks projects into short cycles (iterations) that allow rapid adaptation to changes and continuous quality improvement.

The Four Core Agile Values:

‚Ä¢ Working software over comprehensive documentation

‚Ä¢ Customer collaboration over contract negotiation

‚Ä¢ Responding to change over following a plan

The Scrum Framework

Roles & Responsibilities

Product Owner

Defines product vision and priorities. Manages the Product Backlog and ensures the team delivers maximum value. Acts as the voice of the customer and stakeholders.

üéØ

Scrum Master

Facilitates Scrum processes and removes obstacles. Coaches the team on Agile principles and ensures smooth Sprint execution. Protects the team from external interruptions.

Development Team

Cross-functional, self-organizing group (5-9 members) responsible for delivering potentially shippable increments. Collaborates to design, develop, test, and deploy.

Scrum is the most popular framework for implementing Agile. It relies on small cross-functional teams working in short cycles called Sprints (typically 2-4 weeks). Each Sprint produces a potentially shippable product increment.

Core Components:

‚Ä¢ Product Backlog: Prioritized list of all requirements and features

‚Ä¢ Sprint Backlog: Tasks selected for the current Sprint cycle

‚Ä¢ Increment: Deliverable, working product at Sprint end

‚Ä¢ Definition of Done: Clear criteria for task completion

üí° Real-World Example

In developing an e-commerce app, the Product Owner determines "payment system" is the priority. The Scrum Master ensures the team has all resources needed. The Development Team builds and tests the system during a two-week Sprint.

Sprint Planning

Team selects items from Product Backlog and plans how to complete them. Defines Sprint Goal and creates Sprint Backlog with detailed tasks.

‚òï

Daily Standup

15-minute meeting where each member answers: What did I do yesterday? What will I do today? Any blockers? Promotes transparency and quick issue resolution.

üìä

Sprint Review

Team demonstrates completed work to stakeholders. Gathers feedback on the increment and discusses what to build next based on market changes.

üîÑ

Sprint Retrospective

Team reflects on the Sprint process itself. Discusses what went well, what needs improvement, and creates action items for continuous enhancement.

Each Sprint is a complete work cycle starting with planning and ending with a shippable product. Regular ceremonies ensure transparency, inspection, and adaptation - the three pillars of Scrum.

üí° Real-World Example

An Amazon team working on "Smart Recommendations" selects 5 user stories in Sprint Planning. Daily Standup reveals a developer hit an API issue. In Sprint Review they demo the prototype. In Retrospective they decide to improve communication with the Backend team.
